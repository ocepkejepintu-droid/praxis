# DashClaw

Source: hermes-atlas
Category: Domain Applications
Author: ucsandman

DashClaw is a governance and decision infrastructure layer designed to secure AI agents interacting with real-world systems. It intercepts risky agent actions to evaluate them against declarative policies, routing high-risk tasks for human approval via a dashboard or mobile interface. The system records verifiable evidence for every decision and tracks terminal outcomes to prevent double-execution during agent retries. It integrates with various runtimes including Hermes Agent, Claude Code, and LangChain through MCP servers, SDKs, or lifecycle hooks. Highlights: Evaluates risky agent actions against declarative guard policies; Routes pending approvals to CLI, mobile, or messaging platforms; Prevents double-execution by tracking durable terminal outcomes Decision infrastructure with guard policies and risk assessment for agents
