# avoid-ai-writing

Source: hermes-atlas
Category: Skills & Skill Registries
Author: conorbronsdon

Avoid-ai-writing is a portable skill for AI agents designed to audit and rewrite text to eliminate detectable machine-generated patterns. It utilizes a structured two-pass system that first identifies specific "AI-isms" and then performs a secondary audit to catch any lingering tells in the revised version. The tool relies on a library of 42 pattern categories and a 109-entry word replacement table rather than general prompting. It integrates with Hermes, Claude Code, and OpenClaw, offering both a full rewrite mode and a detection-only mode for content auditing. Highlights: Uses a 109-entry tiered word replacement table for precise editing; Performs two-pass audits to catch recycled transitions and lingering inflation; Detects 42 distinct patterns including significance inflation and copula avoidance Audits and rewrites content to eliminate detectable AI writing patterns
